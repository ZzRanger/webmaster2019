Business & Computers Courses
Business Courses

Business Essentials

This course is designed for the student who seeks an introduction to business, marketing, and management. Students will develop the skills necessary to succeed in the business environment by studying forms of business ownership, functions of management, budgeting and finance, accounting, business communications, law, marketing, and economics. This course is appropriate for any student interested in Entrepreneurship and owning their own business.

Entrepreneurship

This course is designed to teach the student the basic principles and practices of owning a business. Students will learn about planning, organizing, controlling, problem-solving, and decision-making as it relates to owning a small business. Students will explore the free enterprise system through a combination of case studies, group work and speakers.

Marketing

This Technical Level class offers students insight into the processes affecting the flow of goods and services from the producer to the consumer. Topics include market planning, promotion, selling and product management. Project-based learning and virtual business software provide the students with authentic learning opportunities.

Computer Classes

Graphic Design I

Students will acquire knowledge of desktop publishing and page layout design skills. They will incorporate industry-standard desktop publishing software and graphics software as well as other digital media to create flyers, newsletters, brochures, and other publications. Students will create a portfolio of work created in the course.

Graphic Design II

Students will advance their knowledge and skills in Graphic Design with page layout and photo/image manipulation. Students will create basic computer-generated illustrations using a variety of techniques. Course topics include the ways in which visual messages are used in society, the skills needed by a graphic designer and the potential areas of specialization and employment. Students will continue to create a portfolio of work.

Computer Applications I

Students will acquire digital literacy skills essential for success in high school, college, and today’s workforce. Emphasis will be on formatting documents and word processing, spreadsheet, and presentation software. The importance and relevance of merging these platforms will emphasized through the completion of projects. Internet research and electronic communication skills, as well as the ethics related to these skills will be explored.

Computer Applications II

Gaining advanced knowledge and skills in word processing, spreadsheet, and presentation software will be accentuated. Students will become more familiar with personal information management and electronic security, research, and ethics. This course will explore other applications, including (but not limited to) database management, Web 2.0, and desktop publishing. Integrated projects will be completed to help students relate their learning to real-world and relevant situations.
